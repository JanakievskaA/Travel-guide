Paris – Eiffel Tower:
The iconic iron lattice tower on the Champ de Mars offers panoramic views of the city from its three levels. Admission: ~€25. Open daily 09:00–23:00.

Paris – Louvre Museum:
The world's largest art museum, home to the Mona Lisa and thousands of masterpieces. Ticket: ~€17. Hours: 09:00–18:00 (Wednesdays & Fridays until 21:45).

Paris – Notre-Dame Cathedral:
Gothic masterpiece on Île de la Cité, famous for its stained glass and flying buttresses. Free entry; tower climb ticket: ~€10. Open 07:45–18:45.

Paris – Montmartre & Sacré-Cœur:
Charming hilltop neighborhood with artistic heritage and the Basilica offering stunning city vistas. Basilica entry free; dome access: donation-based. Best at sunset.

Paris – Le Marais Food Tour:
Guided walking tour through historic district sampling falafel, pastries, and wine bars. Cost: ~€50. Includes five tastings.

Rome – Colosseum:
Ancient amphitheater where gladiators once battled, an enduring symbol of the Roman Empire. Entry: ~€16. Hours: 08:30–19:00.

Rome – Vatican Museums & Sistine Chapel:
Vast collection of art and frescoes culminating in Michelangelo's Sistine Chapel ceiling. Ticket: ~€17. Hours: 09:00–18:00 (closed Sundays).

Rome – Pantheon:
Well-preserved Roman temple with a striking domed oculus. Free entry. Open 09:00–19:00.

Rome – Trevi Fountain:
Baroque masterpiece where legend says tossing a coin ensures a return to Rome. Free to visit, best before 10:00 or after 21:00 to avoid crowds.

Rome – Trastevere Dining Tour:
A gastronomic stroll in a vibrant neighborhood tasting pasta, pizza, and gelato. Cost: ~€60. Includes four stops.

Tokyo – Senso-ji Temple:
Oldest Buddhist temple in Tokyo, set in the colorful Asakusa district. Free entry; pagoda dome access: small donation. Open 06:00–17:00.

Tokyo – Tsukiji Outer Market:
Bustling market of fresh seafood and street food stalls. Try sushi or grilled skewers for ~¥200–¥500 per item. Open 05:00–14:00.

Tokyo – Shibuya Crossing:
Iconic intersection surrounded by neon billboards and shopping. Free pedestrian scramble; best viewed from a café terrace above the street.

Tokyo – Meiji Shrine & Yoyogi Park:
Shinto shrine nestled in a tranquil forest; adjacent park perfect for a leisurely stroll. Shrine entry free. Park open sunrise–sunset.

Tokyo – Ramen Alley (Tokyo Station):
Narrow corridor of famous ramen shops inside the station offering regional bowls. Prices ~¥800–¥1,200. Open 11:00–23:00.

New York – Statue of Liberty & Ellis Island:
Symbolic copper statue and immigration museum telling America's immigrant story. Ferry + crown access: ~$25. Ferries run 09:00–17:00.

New York – Central Park:
Expansive urban oasis with walking paths, lakes, and cultural landmarks. Free entry; bike rental ~$15/hr. Open daily sunrise–1:00.

New York – The Metropolitan Museum of Art:
Massive art collection spanning 5,000 years and multiple cultures. Suggested admission: ~$30. Hours: 10:00–17:30.

New York – Top of the Rock:
Observation deck atop Rockefeller Center offering 360° views of Manhattan and Central Park. Ticket: ~$38. Hours: 11:00–23:00.

New York – Greenwich Village Food Crawl:
Guided tasting tour of historic neighborhood sampling pizza, bagels, and dessert. Cost: ~$65 for six tastings.

Barcelona – Sagrada Família:
Antoni Gaudí's unfinished basilica renowned for its ornate facades and towering spires. Ticket: ~€26. Hours: 09:00–18:00.

Barcelona – Park Güell:
Colorful mosaic park designed by Gaudí with panoramic city views. Entry: ~€10. Hours: 08:00–21:30.

Barcelona – La Rambla & Boqueria Market:
Famous pedestrian boulevard and vibrant food market offering tapas and fresh produce. Market stalls: €2–€10 per item. Open 08:00–20:30.

Barcelona – Picasso Museum:
Extensive collection of Pablo Picasso's early works in a series of medieval palaces. Ticket: ~€12. Hours: 10:00–19:00.

Barcelona – Tapas Tour in El Born:
Guided tour in trendy district sampling five tapas bars with wine pairings. Cost: ~€55. Duration: 2 hours.

London – Big Ben & Houses of Parliament:
Iconic clock tower and seat of British government along the River Thames. Free to view from outside; guided tours: ~£25. Best viewed from Westminster Bridge.

London – British Museum:
World-renowned museum housing artifacts from around the globe, including the Rosetta Stone and Elgin Marbles. Free entry; special exhibitions: ~£15. Hours: 10:00–17:30.

London – Tower of London:
Historic castle and former royal palace housing the Crown Jewels. Ticket: ~£33. Hours: 09:00–17:30. Beefeater tours included.

London – Buckingham Palace:
Official residence of the British monarch. Changing of the Guard: free, daily at 11:00. State Rooms tour: ~£30 (summer only).

London – Borough Market:
Historic food market offering artisanal produce, street food, and international cuisine. Prices: £5–£15 per meal. Open 10:00–17:00 (closed Sundays).

Sydney – Sydney Opera House:
Iconic performing arts venue with distinctive sail-like design. Guided tours: ~A$43. Best viewed from Circular Quay or Harbour Bridge.

Sydney – Sydney Harbour Bridge:
Steel arch bridge offering panoramic views of the harbor. BridgeClimb: ~A$268. Pylon Lookout: ~A$19. Pedestrian walkway: free.

Sydney – Bondi Beach:
Famous surf beach with golden sand and excellent waves. Free access. Beach walk to Coogee: 6km coastal path with stunning views.

Sydney – Royal Botanic Gardens:
Lush gardens overlooking Sydney Harbour with native and exotic plants. Free entry. Open 07:00–20:00. Guided tours available.

Sydney – The Rocks Markets:
Weekend markets in historic area featuring local crafts, food, and souvenirs. Prices: A$5–A$50. Open Saturdays 10:00–17:00, Sundays 09:00–17:00.

Dubai – Burj Khalifa:
World's tallest building with observation decks on 124th and 148th floors. At the Top: ~AED 149. At the Top Sky: ~AED 378. Hours: 08:30–23:00.

Dubai – Dubai Mall:
Massive shopping and entertainment complex with over 1,200 stores, aquarium, and ice rink. Free entry; activities: AED 50–AED 200. Open 10:00–24:00.

Dubai – Palm Jumeirah:
Artificial palm-shaped island with luxury resorts and beaches. Monorail: ~AED 25. Beach access: varies by resort. Best viewed from helicopter tour: ~AED 500.

Dubai – Dubai Marina:
Modern waterfront district with skyscrapers, restaurants, and boat tours. Dhow cruise: ~AED 50–AED 150. Dinner cruise: ~AED 200. Evening tours available.

Dubai – Gold Souk:
Traditional market in Deira selling gold, jewelry, and spices. Free entry; prices negotiable. Open 10:00–22:00 (closed Fridays 13:30–16:00).

Amsterdam – Rijksmuseum:
National museum showcasing Dutch art and history, including Rembrandt's "Night Watch". Ticket: ~€22.50. Hours: 09:00–17:00.

Amsterdam – Anne Frank House:
Museum in the hiding place where Anne Frank wrote her diary. Ticket: ~€16 (advance booking required). Hours: 09:00–22:00. Very popular, book weeks ahead.

Amsterdam – Van Gogh Museum:
Largest collection of Van Gogh's paintings and drawings. Ticket: ~€20. Hours: 09:00–18:00. Audio guide included.

Amsterdam – Canal Cruise:
Scenic boat tour through Amsterdam's historic canal ring. Ticket: ~€16–€25. Duration: 1–1.5 hours. Multiple departure points. Best in daylight.

Amsterdam – Jordaan District:
Charming neighborhood with narrow streets, art galleries, and cozy cafes. Free to explore. Best for walking and discovering local life.

Singapore – Marina Bay Sands:
Iconic hotel with rooftop infinity pool and SkyPark observation deck. SkyPark entry: ~S$26. Hours: 09:30–22:00. Spectacular views of the city.

Singapore – Gardens by the Bay:
Futuristic park with Supertree Grove and climate-controlled conservatories. Free entry to outdoor gardens. Cloud Forest & Flower Dome: ~S$28. Open 05:00–02:00.

Singapore – Sentosa Island:
Resort island with beaches, theme parks, and attractions. Free entry; attractions vary: S$10–S$80. Monorail: ~S$4. Best for families.

Singapore – Singapore Zoo:
World-class zoo with open enclosures and animal shows. Ticket: ~S$48. Hours: 08:30–18:00. Night Safari available separately: ~S$55.

Singapore – Hawker Centers:
Food courts offering diverse Asian cuisine at affordable prices. Popular: Maxwell Food Centre, Lau Pa Sat. Prices: S$3–S$15 per meal. Open various hours.
